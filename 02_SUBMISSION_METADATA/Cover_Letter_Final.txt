Dear Editor,

Please consider the manuscript entitled “TAFP-GraphRAG: An Empirical Topology-Aware Framework for Protecting Sensitive Relations in Graph Retrieval-Augmented Generation” for publication in the Journal of Information Security and Applications.

The manuscript is not under consideration for publication elsewhere.

The manuscript presents an empirical, pre-indexing GraphRAG protection framework that integrates policy-guided concealment of sensitive relation semantics, exact per-node degree preservation in designated variants, auditable execution, and attack-specific privacy–utility evaluation. The evaluation separates relation-label concealment, structural inference, released-view attacks, marker leakage, structural retrieval utility, and retrieval-grounded relation question answering. The manuscript explicitly limits its claims to the evaluated protocols and does not claim formal differential privacy or universal protection.

We believe the work is relevant to JISA because it addresses an applied information-security problem at the intersection of graph retrieval, privacy-aware system design, adversarial evaluation, and reproducible security auditing.

The manuscript contains the required funding, competing-interest, ethics, data-availability, code-availability, and AI-use declarations. Supplementary material and hash-verified reproducibility records accompany the submission package.

Thank you for your consideration.
